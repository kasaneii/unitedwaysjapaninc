Cookie Policy

This policy is effective as of 17th June 2025

At United Ways Japan Inc., we use cookies and similar technologies to enhance your browsing experience, analyze site traffic, and personalize content. This Cookies Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, and your choices regarding their use.

1. What is a Cookie?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites recognize your device and remember information about your visit, such as your preferences or login status.

2. What Types of Cookies Are There?

We use different types of cookies to serve various purposes. Below is a breakdown of the types of cookies that may be used on our website:

2.1. Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable basic features such as page navigation, access to secure areas, and form submissions. Without these cookies, some parts of the site may not work correctly.

Examples include:

  • Session management

  • Security validation

  • Account login functionality

  • These cookies do not store any personally identifiable information and cannot be switched off in our systems.

2.2. Performance Cookies

Performance cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. This includes data such as the number of visitors, the pages visited, and how users navigate the site.

We use this information to:

  • Improve website performance and usability

  • Identify and fix issues quickly

  • Measure the effectiveness of our content and layout

These cookies are optional and can be disabled without affecting basic functionality.

2.3. Functionality Cookies

These cookies allow the website to remember choices you make, such as language preferences, region, or customized settings. They enhance your experience by providing more personalized features and smoother navigation.

Functionality cookies may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we‚Äôve added to our pages.

Disabling these cookies may result in reduced functionality or a less personalized experience.

3. Third-Party Cookies on Our Site

Some cookies may be set by third-party services we use, such as analytics tools (e.g., Google Analytics) or embedded content. These third parties may collect information about your browsing behavior, subject to their own privacy policies.

4. Cookies and Your Personal Information

Some cookies collect data that may be considered personal information under applicable privacy laws. This may include your IP address, browser type, device information, and usage data when you interact with our website.

We use this information to:

  • Improve your browsing experience

  • Analyze site usage and performance

  • Personalize content and offers relevant to you

All data collected through cookies is handled in accordance with our Privacy Policy, which outlines how we store, use, and protect your personal information.

5. How to Configure and Disable Cookies

You can manage your cookie preferences at any time by adjusting your browser settings. Most web browsers allow you to:

  • View the cookies stored on your device

  • Delete cookies individually or all at once

  • Block or allow cookies on specific websites

  • Set preferences for how cookies are accepted or rejected

Please note that disabling certain cookies, especially essential or functionality cookies, may affect your ability to use some features on our website.

To find out more about managing cookies, you can refer to the help section of your browser or visit websites like www.allaboutcookies.org.
